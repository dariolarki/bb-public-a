Research Note: Urban Tree Canopy Cooling

Urban tree canopy coverage can reduce pedestrian heat exposure by providing shade, lowering surface temperatures, and improving perceived comfort during peak summer hours.

Cities that combine shade trees with reflective surfaces and access to drinking water often see better outcomes than cities that rely on a single intervention.

A practical planning approach is to prioritize bus stops, school routes, senior centers, and high-foot-traffic corridors with limited shade.
