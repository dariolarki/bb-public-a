Urban Heat Resilience Brief

Urban tree canopy programs can reduce pedestrian heat exposure by increasing shade, lowering pavement temperatures, and improving comfort during peak summer hours.

Cooling plans are strongest when shade trees are paired with reflective surfaces, drinking water access, and shaded waiting areas near transit.

Planners often prioritize school routes, bus stops, senior centers, and high-foot-traffic corridors where heat exposure and shade gaps overlap.
